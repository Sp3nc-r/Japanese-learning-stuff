漢字、中国語: Japanese Kanji, What Do They Mean?

When learning Japanese, the kanji are by far the hardest part. Most children don't even use it! In this article, the reader will learn about some basic kanji, and their meanings.

Some basic kanji

  • 何 (nani) - What
  • 夜 (yoru) - Night(time)
  • 昼 (hiru) - Day(time)
  • ご飯 (gohan) - Meal/Rice

You can combine kanji to make new words! For example, combine 昼 and ご飯 to get お昼ご飯, which means lunch! It makes sense, daytime + meal = daytime meal = lunch!

Other kanji

There are >50,000 kanji in Japanese. For reference, a well read expert knows but a tenth of that number.


Source

Japanese kanji are based on Chinese characters, which is why the language is constantly mistaken for Chinese.
